PHILOSOPHY IN LIFE

Life itself is the essence of mind and matter as per René Descartes. "Cogito, ergo sum”, “I think, therefore I am”, this simple philosophy has deep meanings that really spoke to me on a fundamental level. The dualism of existence, mind and matter how we all came to be. The mind can exist without the body but the body would fall short lest there is mind. The metaphysics that encompasses this idea has made me rethink what it is to live. To us, thinking is a natural occurrence, like breathing, blinking, and all those autonomous systems within our body. But to be able to think means that we are in this world. Whenever I doubt myself, I sit and think. I always come to the conclusion that because I can have cognitive function, therefore I can exist in this world. Meaning, whenever I face tough challenges that puts me on my backfoot, I stop and think. This erases all my doubts and fears, which propels me to formulate what I can do in order to solve my problems. I use this philosophy during this rigorous training. Stepping back and seeing the big picture, looking at the details I missed and carefully concocting a thought. Being in this state allows me to believe in myself and that is the grounded truth in the simple thought of “I think, therefore I am”.


CAREER PLAN

Adulthood was something I had a hard time grasping during my childhood. I never really had any plans or dreams like the other kids who was my age did. I’ve lived life one day, one hour, one second at a time. But alas, time was but a fleeting concept that which binds us to this temporal line that only goes forward. And with that, I realized that I had to at least come up with a plan for my future. As I graduate from OMSC, I had realized a version of myself. One that which has an idea on what to pursue as my career. Applying to all available jobs that are within my skillset while also honing my talents and skills further. This plan is not really concrete since the future is dynamic and a lot of things can happen so I treat this idea as my guideline. If possible, I want to use my knowledge to help people while also making money. I would also like to experience the whole world and what it can offer. I know myself as one who doesn’t turn down an opportunity to try new things and I intend to keep it that way for my career as well. As I wrap up my thoughts, the opportunity that this program has given me expanded the opportunities I can take in the future. BSIT is what I can think as one of the flexible programs as it deals with technology. I think that I’ve learned enough to say that I can pursue any field that I can think of as long as I can use the knowledge, I’ve accumulated from all the years of my education. A true master is forever a student, to continue learning even while pursuing a career is my plan.


A. IMPORTANCE OF INTERNSHIP

The importance of internship is to have the opportunity to experience what its like to be in a working environment. To build a habit and proper work ethics. Internship is like a interactive window to the real working environment that gives us limited access to feel what its like to work. The most important thing is to prepare us as we go on to step outside our comfort zone.


B. OBJECTIVES OF INTERNSHIP

The objectives of an internship are to help develop the important skills of the intern by providing practical experiences in controlled or some less than controlled environment or conditions. The interns will naturally develop into a semi-professional, qualified to take on tasks that are at their level or sometimes even higher. It also develops their ability to communicate with adults during working hours.

The Future of Immersive Web Experiences

May 2, 2025 • 5 min read

The web is evolving beyond traditional 2D interfaces into fully immersive experiences that engage all senses. As WebXR and spatial computing become more accessible, we're seeing a shift in how users interact with digital content.

Recent advancements in browser-based rendering engines have made it possible to create experiences that were previously only possible in native applications. Three.js and WebGPU are leading this revolution, allowing developers to implement complex 3D worlds with physics simulations and realistic lighting directly in the browser.

As someone who works at the intersection of design and development, I find these possibilities incredibly exciting. The challenge now is to create experiences that aren't just technically impressive but also meaningful and accessible to users across different devices and abilities.

Designing with Ethical AI: Principles and Practices

April 15, 2025 • 8 min read

AI is now an integral part of the design process, but with great power comes great responsibility. How do we ensure that our AI-assisted design tools are ethical, unbiased, and respectful of human creativity?

In my recent work, I've been exploring frameworks for ethical AI implementation in creative processes. One approach involves transparent documentation of where AI has been used in a project, acknowledging both its contributions and limitations.

Another important practice is maintaining human oversight over generated content, especially when dealing with culturally sensitive material or content aimed at vulnerable populations. This isn't about limiting AI's capabilities, but rather about thoughtfully integrating them into a human-centered design process.

I believe that the future belongs not to AI alone, nor to human designers working in isolation, but to thoughtful collaboration between the two, leveraging the strengths of each while compensating for their respective weaknesses.

Creating Accessible Animations: Performance and Inclusion

March 30, 2025 • 6 min read

Dynamic animations can bring interfaces to life, but they can also create barriers for users with motion sensitivity or those on lower-end devices. How do we balance expressiveness with accessibility?

In my recent project for a healthcare client, I implemented a system of animation preferences that allows users to specify their comfort level with motion. This isn't just an on/off toggle – it's a spectrum that adjusts the intensity, speed, and complexity of animations throughout the application.

From a technical perspective, this required rethinking how animations are structured in our codebase. We created an animation orchestration layer that could dynamically adjust parameters based on user preferences and device capabilities.

The results were impressive: engagement increased across all user groups, including those who had previously avoided interactive features due to motion concerns. This project reinforced my belief that accessibility constraints often lead to more innovative and elegant solutions that benefit all users.

DevOps Outsourcing Specializes in Business Process Outsourcing. Helping clients or companies to leverage technology in order to make their workloads faster and more reliable.
